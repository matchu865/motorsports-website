About Vanderbilt Motorsports

What Do We Do?

The Vanderbilt University Motorsports Team designs, builds, and competes small formula style cars for FSAE® competitions. Currently, the fabrication of our 2015 car is well underway, and we are eagerly preparing for competition.

What is Baja SAE?

The goal in Baja SAE racing is to design, build and race off-road vehicles that can withstand the harshest elements of rough terrain. The vehicles used in Baja SAE racing are often similar in appearance to dune buggies. Before 2007, the events were called "Mini Baja."

What is Formula SAE?

Formula SAE is an international engineering competition sponsored by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE). This competition presents students with the challenge of designing and building a small open wheel racecar, evaluated for its feasibility to be sold as a weekend autocross vehicle. During the school year students design and fabricate their car before bringing it to compete against other universities at competitions in Michigan, as well as seven other international sites. At competition, students are judged in the areas of design, cost, business presentation, and racing performance. Adeptness in the areas of design, fabrication, teamwork, leadership, and marketing are vital to a team’s success. Ultimately, participation in FSAE gives students an edge upon graduating by exposing them to these skills that are unattainable in the classroom. The program has gained significant reputation, and many employers focus primarily on recruiting FSAE graduates.
